Batman

I am vengeance, I am the night, I AM BATMAN!
Batman, also known as Bruce Wayne, is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by DC Comics. The character was created by artist Bob Kane and writer Bill Finger, and first appeared in Detective Comics #27 in 1939. Batman is a vigilante who operates in the fictional Gotham City, and is commonly depicted as a physically fit, intelligent, and wealthy individual who uses his intellect, detective skills, martial arts abilities, and an extensive array of high-tech gadgets to combat crime. Batman's origin story is that as a child, he witnessed the murder of his parents, Thomas and Martha Wayne, by a mugger named Joe Chill. This trauma led him to train himself to fight crime and seek justice. Batman is often associated with a number of iconic elements, including his costume, the Batmobile, the Batcave, and his rogues gallery of villains, which includes the Joker, Catwoman, the Penguin, Two-Face, and the Riddler. Batman has been adapted into various media, including film, television, and video games, and has become one of the most popular and enduring superheroes in the world.

Batman Villains

Batman has a rogues gallery of iconic villains that have appeared in his comics over the years. Here are some of the most notable ones:

The Joker is Batman's most famous and iconic villain. He first appeared in Batman #1 in 1940 and was created by Bob Kane, Bill Finger, and Jerry Robinson. The Joker is a psychopathic criminal mastermind who uses chaos and anarchy to spread fear and terror in Gotham City.

Catwoman, also known as Selina Kyle, is a cat burglar and Batman's love interest. She first appeared in Batman #1 in 1940 and was created by Bob Kane and Bill Finger. Catwoman is a complex character who has been both a villain and an anti-hero in the Batman universe.

The Penguin, also known as Oswald Cobblepot, is a criminal mastermind who uses umbrellas as weapons. He first appeared in Detective Comics #58 in 1941 and was created by Bob Kane and Bill Finger. The Penguin is known for his distinctive appearance and his love for birds.

Two-Face, also known as Harvey Dent, is a former district attorney who became a criminal after half of his face was scarred by acid. He first appeared in Detective Comics #66 in 1942 and was created by Bob Kane and Bill Finger. Two-Face is obsessed with duality and makes decisions based on the flip of a coin.

The Riddler, also known as Edward Nigma, is a criminal mastermind who leaves riddles and puzzles for Batman to solve. He first appeared in Detective Comics #140 in 1948 and was created by Bill Finger and Dick Sprang. The Riddler is known for his green suit covered in question marks and his love for riddles.

Poison Ivy, also known as Pamela Isley, is a botanist who became a villain after being exposed to toxins that gave her control over plants. She first appeared in Batman #181 in 1966 and was created by Robert Kanigher and Sheldon Moldoff. Poison Ivy is known for her love of plants and her ability to control them.
